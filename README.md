# SentinelPaper

## AI-Powered Forensic Examination Security Platform

### Every Leak Leaves a Fingerprint.

SentinelPaper is an AI-powered examination security platform designed to combat one of the largest integrity challenges in education: examination paper leaks.

Instead of relying solely on prevention, SentinelPaper introduces forensic accountability into the examination lifecycle. Every generated examination paper contains a unique cryptographic identity that enables authorities to trace leaked content back to its source within seconds.

The platform combines AI-assisted examination generation, forensic watermarking, leak attribution, audit intelligence, and chain-of-custody tracking into a unified security ecosystem.

---

## Problem Statement

Examination paper leaks impact millions of students every year.

Current systems focus primarily on access control and operational secrecy. Once a paper is leaked, investigators often struggle to determine:

* Which examination center leaked the paper
* Which operator handled the document
* Which batch was compromised
* When the leak occurred
* Whether multiple leaks are connected

The result is delayed investigations, canceled examinations, financial losses, legal disputes, and loss of public trust.

SentinelPaper addresses this gap by making every examination paper uniquely identifiable.

---

## Solution

SentinelPaper generates examination papers embedded with forensic identifiers that survive distribution, printing, scanning, screenshots, and digital forwarding.

If a leaked image or document appears online, investigators can upload it into SentinelPaper.

The platform automatically:

1. Detects forensic markers
2. Extracts hidden identifiers
3. Maps the document to its source
4. Calculates confidence scores
5. Generates a forensic investigation report

This transforms examination security from reactive investigation to proactive accountability.

## Innovation

Most examination security systems focus on preventing leaks.

SentinelPaper focuses on making leaks traceable.

This creates a powerful deterrence mechanism:

"If a paper leaks, the source can be identified."

The system transforms examination documents into forensic assets rather than static PDFs.
